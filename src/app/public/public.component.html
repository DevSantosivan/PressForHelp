How It Works

911 is your direct connection to police, fire, and medical emergency services. This system ensures that emergency responders are dispatched as quickly as possible to provide assistance.

When you call 911, your call is routed to the nearest Public Safety Answering Point (PSAP). Trained dispatchers gather key information from you, assess the situation, and send the necessary help based on your location and type of emergency.

Call 911 for life-threatening emergencies, crimes in progress, fires, medical emergencies, and accidents. If you are unsure whether your situation qualifies, err on the side of caution and call—dispatchers will guide you.
